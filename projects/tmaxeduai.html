01. Problem

One-size-fits-all learning content ignores where each student actually is. To personalize it, you need to answer two hard questions well: what does this learner currently know? and what should they do next?

Both are hard in practice. Estimating a knowledge state breaks down for new learners with little history (the cold-start problem), and turning that estimate into the right recommendation has to hold up at scale, in a live multi-tenant service.

02. What I built

  1. Knowledge tracing

    Designed a method to estimate students' knowledge states from their problem-solving history, explicitly handling cold-start for new learners. In a separate proof of concept, trained a deep-learning knowledge-tracing model on history from 700+ students.

  2. Recommendation

    Built rule-based problem recommendation driven by knowledge-state levels for the digital textbook, and a deep-learning personalized recommender on sequential interaction history and user profiles for the large-scale learning platform.

  3. Production & MLOps

    Shipped these into production through Airflow pipelines for scheduled training and batch inference, with pre/post-processing to keep recommendations valid in a multi-tenant setting, served via RESTful APIs.
