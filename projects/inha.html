Construction Site Hazard Detection

Image classification and object detection to flag small- and medium-sized hazards in indoor construction sites — part of a research effort to automate safety-rule checking.

Role
Undergraduate Researcher · Construction Systems Lab, Inha University
Timeline
2019 – 2020
Stack
Python · Deep Learning · Computer Vision
Focus
Object detection · Image classification · Data augmentation

01. Problem

Indoor construction sites are full of small- and medium-sized objects that can pose safety risks, and they're easy for human inspectors to miss. The lab's broader goal was to automate safety-rule checking — which first requires reliably detecting those objects from site imagery.

That detection is the hard part: small and medium objects are tough to localize, and labeled construction-site imagery is scarce, so models struggle to generalize.

02. What I did

  1. Detection & classification models

    Built and trained image classification and object detection models to identify safety-relevant small- and medium-sized objects from indoor site imagery.

  2. Image augmentation for scarce data

    Studied and applied image-augmentation techniques aimed at improving recognition of small- and medium-sized objects under limited labeled data.

  3. Experimentation

    Ran experiments comparing classification and detection approaches to understand what worked best for this setting, feeding the results back into the automated safety-rule-checking project.

03. Context

This was my first hands-on research in machine learning, during an undergraduate internship at the Construction Systems Laboratory. It's where I first saw a model move from an experiment to something with a real, physical purpose — and what got me to pursue ML seriously.
